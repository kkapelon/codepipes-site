Really old articles in Operating System News

More polished that Slashdot and more focused in operating systems, OS News was one of my favourite sites as a CS student/graduate. Over the years I published 5 opinion articles there.

The Rise (And Fall?) of Application Stores (2011)

This article came as a reaction of the huge success of the Apple appstore where developers give 30% of their earnings to Apple. Even though app stores are here to stay, I still think that they are a step to the wrong direction as far as software distribution is concerned. Read the full article - The Rise (And Fall?) of Application Stores .

Enlightenment Libraries Reach 1.0 (2011)

The Enlightenment foundation libraries were in beta for so long that nobody believed the release anouncement. I had abandoned them because they lacked a stable release. Nowadays enlightenment is not as impressive as it used to be, and it is mostly interesting to developers of low powered devices. Read the full article - Enlightenment Libraries Reach 1.0 .

Moving Away From WIMP Interfaces One Step at a Time (2006)

My initial announcment of the (now abandoned) Project Elevate. Read the full article - Moving Away From WIMP Interfaces One Step at a Time .

The desktop is changing but to what? (2006)

A discussion on the state of Linux Window Environments circa 2006. Read the full article - The desktop is changing but to what? .
